Call for Data

Help Build LabOS for Scientific Vision and Reasoning

LabOS is building vision-language models for real-world wet-lab understanding. We are inviting researchers, labs, institutions, and collaborators to contribute high-quality multimodal data that can help train and evaluate the next generation of scientific AI systems. Our focus is on data that captures real laboratory procedures, visual manipulation, protocol execution, and error detection in authentic wet-lab environments. We welcome contributions that can help LabOS better understand what scientists do, what they see, and how experimental workflows unfold in practice.

What We Are Looking For

We are seeking wet-lab datasets that include one or more of the following:

  • First-person or third-person laboratory videos
  • Image or video data aligned with written experimental protocols
  • Step-by-step annotations of laboratory procedures
  • Error or issue annotations during execution
  • Multi-view recordings of the same procedure
  • Supporting metadata such as protocol name, scene type, point of view, or task category
  • Optional audio-aligned or outcome-aligned recordings
Priority will be given to data that reflects real experimental workflows rather than heavily staged demonstrations.

Preferred Data Characteristics

We strongly encourage submissions with:

  • Clear visibility of both the operator and the object being manipulated
  • Diverse camera angles and viewpoints
  • Varied subject positions and scales within the frame
  • Realistic wet-lab tasks involving hand-object interaction
  • Multiple perspectives of the same procedure when available
  • Protocol-aligned recordings with step-level correspondence
  • Both successful runs and runs containing common mistakes or deviations
  • Natural laboratory environments, including clutter, occlusion, and real-world variability
For LabOS, diversity in viewpoint matters. Egocentric views, bench-side views, rear views, top-down views, and fixed environmental views can all be valuable when they help capture the procedure clearly.

Recommended Capture Setup

You do not need a specialized studio setup to contribute.

Accepted capture devices may include:

  • Smart glasses
  • Action cameras
  • Smartphones
  • Webcams
  • Fixed lab cameras

A single camera is acceptable, but multi-view recording is preferred whenever possible.

At minimum, at least one camera should maintain a clear view of both the human subject and the object or sample being manipulated throughout the procedure.

Examples of useful viewpoints include:

  • Egocentric view from head-mounted glasses or cameras
  • Bench-side view from the left or right
  • Rear view behind the operator
  • Chest-mounted view for close manipulation
  • Top-down or overhead view
  • Robotic wrist or system-mounted view for automated workflows

Data Quality Expectations

To be useful for model training, submissions should aim for:

  • Stable recording with sufficient resolution
  • Clear visual access to key actions, tools, and materials
  • Minimal heavy filtering or post-processing
  • Consistent framing during important steps
  • Accurate protocol alignment
  • High-quality annotations when provided
  • Appropriate documentation of missing, obscured, or failed steps

We especially value data that helps models distinguish between:

  • visible actions
  • contextual waiting or setup states
  • errors or deviations
  • steps that occurred but were not visible

Annotation and Metadata

Annotated data is highly valuable.

If possible, please include:

  • Protocol title
  • Step-level timestamps
  • Action labels
  • Context or state labels
  • Error annotations
  • Notes for steps that were not visible
  • Point-of-view information
  • Device type
  • Recording date
  • Site or lab identifier
  • Outcome or result references, when appropriate
Structured formats such as .txt or .json are preferred for annotations and metadata.

What to Include in Your Submission

A complete submission may include:

  • Raw video or image files
  • Reference protocol or task document
  • Aligned annotations
  • Error labels, if applicable
  • Basic metadata and file descriptions
  • Information on capture device and camera position
  • Permission and usage rights confirmation

If your dataset includes multiple views, please indicate how the different feeds correspond to the same session.

Rights, Permissions, and Safety

Please only submit data that you are authorized to share.

By submitting data, you confirm that:

  • you have the right to share the data
  • the data may be used for research and model development
  • the submission does not violate privacy, confidentiality, or institutional policy
  • any required permissions or consents have been obtained

Please do not submit data containing sensitive personal information, restricted materials, or content you are not authorized to distribute.

Why Contribute

Your data can help build scientific AI systems that are better at:

  • understanding wet-lab procedures
  • aligning visual activity with written protocols
  • detecting mistakes and deviations
  • improving reproducibility
  • supporting real-time scientific assistance

We believe progress in laboratory AI depends on high-quality, real-world multimodal data contributed by the scientific community.

Submission Process

To submit data, please prepare:

  1. Your files and folders
  2. A short description of the procedure or dataset
  3. Protocol and annotation files, if available
  4. Metadata for device, viewpoint, and task type
  5. A note describing any known errors, deviations, or visibility limitations
